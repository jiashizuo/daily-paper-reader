## 摘要
细胞活动对大规模脑动力学的影响被认为决定了脑功能与疾病，然而跨尺度神经机制的因果关系仍不清楚。最近，有报道称小脑在感觉运动整合过程中影响全脑动力学。为揭示其潜在机制，我们开发了一种新型多尺度数字脑协同模拟器，其中将橄榄-小脑微电路的脉冲神经网络嵌入虚拟小鼠大脑，并使用基于图谱的长程连接组与其他节点连接。脉冲橄榄-小脑网络与其他速率编码模块之间的参数和双向接口经过调整，以匹配初级感觉和运动皮层（M1和S1）功率谱密度以及小脑神经元脉冲率的实验数据。然后，通过模拟损伤关键电路连接，分析了小脑回路在感觉运动整合中的作用。模拟识别出微电路内驱动M1-S1相干性的特定通路，以及浦肯野细胞抑制通过去相关小脑核活动来增强小脑引擎处理效率的作用。这些结果提供了小脑如何促进皮层模块中感觉运动偶然性形成的机制解释，为其预测能力奠定基础。这种新颖的建模方法为研究与特定细胞和微电路特性相关的脑生理和病理状态的多尺度研究开辟了新视角。

## Abstract
The impact of cellular activities on large-scale brain dynamics is thought to determine brain functioning and disease, yet the causal relationships of neural mechanisms across scales remain unclear. Recently, the cerebellum has been reported to affect whole-brain dynamics during sensorimotor integration. To disclose the underlying mechanisms, we developed a novel multiscale digital brain co-simulator, in which a spiking neural network of the olivo-cerebellar microcircuit is embedded in a virtual mouse brain and wired with other nodes using an atlas-based long-range connectome. Parameters and bi-directional interfaces between the spiking olivo-cerebellar network and other rate-coded modules were tuned to match experimental data of primary sensory and motor cortex (M1 and S1) power spectral densities and neuronal cerebellar spiking rates. Then, the role of the cerebellar circuitry on sensorimotor integration was analyzed by lesioning critical circuit connections in silico. Simulations identified the specific pathway inside the microcircuit driving M1-S1 coherence, and the role of Purkinje cell inhibition in enhancing the processing efficiency of the cerebellar engine through decorrelation of cerebellar nuclei activity. These results provide a mechanistic explanation of how the cerebellum promotes the formation of sensorimotor contingencies in cortical modules setting the basis for its predictive capability. This novel modelling approach opens new perspectives for the multiscale investigation of brain physiological and pathological states in relation to specific cellular and microcircuit properties.
