Summary 20 

Genomics is adopting autonomous AI agents that interpret genomes from natural-21 language instructions faster than it is building the means to trust them. We report the 22 first large-scale controlled evaluation of where, in an agentic genomic pipeline, 23 correctness must reside for the system to be trustworthy at clinical scale. Using 24 pharmacogenomics, a domain where errors are measurable and sometimes lethal, we 25 benchmarked nine frontier large language models across 44,550 scored evaluations on 26 110 pharmacogenomic cases, and tested model interpretation of real star-allele 27 diplotypes from more than 7,000 individuals in three ancestrally diverse populations. 28 Trustworthiness proved to be a property of pipeline architecture, not of the model. 29 Letting the model reason was stochastic and unsafe, and grounding it in the correct 30 guidelines by retrieval paradoxically increased lethal-class errors. Encoding the 31 validated decision logic as a versioned skill and executing it as code made the 32 pharmacogenomic mapping exact, auditable and identical across models, confining all 33 residual error to a single input-interpretation step. On individual genomes, unguarded 34 model interpretation degraded along an ancestry gradient; execution removes this 35 gradient from the clinical mapping, relocating it to the auditable completeness of the 36 input caller. This establishes a generalisable, auditable architecture for trustworthy 37 agentic genome interpretation at scale. 38 39

Significance 53 

Genomics is adopting autonomous, language-model-mediated agents faster than it is 54 building the standards needed to trust them. On a pharmacogenomic benchmark with 55 lethal-class consequences, we show that an agent’s trustworthiness is not a property of 56 the model but of how the agent is constrained: correctness must be moved out of the 57 stochastic model into a versioned skill executed as code, with the model confined to 58 interpreting heterogeneous input. This gives the field a transferable architecture for 59 trustworthy agentic genome interpretation, a predicted route to deploying it so that every 60 emitted answer is correct (execute the validated skill, call the input deterministically, and 61 abstain on the irreducible residual), and a way to develop genomic skills as validated, 62 executable, versioned units rather than prompts. Following a validation framework 63 described elsewhere, we use clinical-grade to mean determinism, auditability, 64 traceability to versioned components and population-invariant performance, all achieved 65 under skill-constrained execution. We distinguish two senses of population 66 performance: the executed clinical mapping is population-invariant by construction, 67 verified across European, Latin American and East African origin individuals, whereas 68 the model’s interpretation of real, ancestrally diverse diplotypes is not, degrading along 69 an ancestry gradient, which is precisely why the mapping must be executed rather than 70 reasoned. We do not claim full clinical validation, which would additionally require non-71 canonical inputs, real-world genomic and clinical data, human comparators and multi-72 site concordance. 73 74 

Introduction 75 

Genomics has entered a phase in which AI agents can autonomously discover, 76 configure, execute and chain bioinformatics operations from natural-language 77 instructions (1). This paradigm, agentic genomics, shifts the bottleneck in computational 78 biology from building pipelines to validating them (1), and with it comes a new class of 79 risk: agents can generate results faster than researchers can check them, and the most 80 dangerous errors are the ones that look plausible but are wrong. A companion 81 Perspective to this paper (1) sets out a tiered validation framework for agentic genomics 82 and identifies the clinical-grade tier as the most demanding, requiring outputs that are 83 deterministic, auditable, traceable to specific versioned components, and population-84 invariant. Here we operationalise that framework on a single clinical-task substrate, 85 pharmacogenomic interpretation, to ask the question that decides whether agentic 86 genome interpretation can be automated at scale: where in an agentic pipeline must 87 correctness reside for the system to be trustworthy? 88 89 Two answers dominate practice. The first is to let the model reason from its training 90 prior. The second, retrieval-augmented generation, grounds the model in retrieved 91 evidence and lets it reason over that (2, 3, 4); large language models encode 92 substantial clinical knowledge (5), and the reflex in biology is to surface the relevant 93 guidelines and let the model apply them. This paper tests a third, structural answer: 94

In the agentic genomics 95 framework, a skill is a self-contained, versioned unit of bioinformatics functionality 96 whose declarative specification (SKILL.md) defines the analytical logic, expected inputs 97 and outputs, and domain-expert decisions (1, 6). A skill can be used in two ways: the 98 model can read its rules and reason to an answer, or the validated logic can be 99 executed as code with the model confined to supplying input. That distinction turns out 100 to be decisive. 101 102 Pharmacogenomics is a rigorous test domain: the genotype-to-phenotype-to-103 recommendation mapping is well-defined by the Clinical Pharmacogenetics 104 Implementation Consortium (CPIC) Level A guideline corpus (7), errors are clinically 105 measurable, and the task demands precise adherence to published guidance. The 106 benchmark includes high-stakes drug-gene pairs with documented lethal 107 consequences: DPYD variants where standard fluoropyrimidine dosing causes severe 108 or fatal toxicity in approximately 0.1 to 0.5% of carriers (8); TPMT and NUDT15 109 deficiencies where standard thiopurine dosing causes lethal myelosuppression (9); 110 CYP2C19 poor metabolisers where standard clopidogrel dosing fails to prevent stent 111 thrombosis (10); and HLA risk alleles where exposure to specific drugs causes Stevens-112 Johnson syndrome, toxic epidermal necrolysis or hypersensitivity reaction (11, 12). 113 114 Concurrent work applies agents to genomic tasks but does not address this question. 115 Recent systems generate pharmacogenomic recommendations from the literature 116 through an agentic pipeline (13), or prioritise rare-disease variants for diagnosis at scale 117 (14); both build end-to-end pipelines and report task accuracy. Neither isolates where in 118 the pipeline correctness must reside, neither separates a model that reasons over a skill 119 from one that executes it as code, and neither establishes determinism, auditability and 120 model-invariance as properties of execution rather than of the model. That controlled 121 dissociation, not the construction of another agentic system, is our contribution. 122 123 We benchmark nine frontier large language models along a five-step constraint gradient 124 that progressively relocates correctness from the model into an executed skill: (i) free-125 prompted; (ii) retrieval-augmented from the CPIC corpus; (iii) skill-reasoning, in which 126 the model applies a versioned skill’s rules and generates the answer; (iv) skill-127 execution, in which the model supplies a structured input and the validated skill 128 computes the answer in code; and (v) an answer-supplied positive control. The gradient 129 shows that trustworthiness is a property of where correctness sits, not of the model: the 130 unconstrained and retrieval-grounded conditions are stochastic and unsafe; structured 131 skills make capable models reliable and near model-invariant; execution makes the 132 clinical mapping exact and confines all residual error to one auditable step; and a 133 validated deterministic caller for that step closes the pipeline toward complete 134 correctness among emitted in-scope answers. We frame the contribution as an 135 architecture and an evaluation for trustworthy agentic genome interpretation at scale, 136 not as a claim that models become clinically correct in isolation. Results 154 

A constraint-gradient benchmark of agentic pharmacogenomic interpretation 155 

We benchmarked nine frontier large language models (Claude Opus 4, Claude Sonnet 156 4, GPT-5.2, GPT-4.1, o3, o4-mini, Gemini 2.5 Flash, DeepSeek V3, Mistral Large 2) on 157 110 CPIC Level A cases spanning 21 markers and 35 gene-drug pairs. The three core 158 conditions (free-prompted, retrieval-augmented, answer-supplied) were evaluated 159 across three population contexts and three replicates, producing 26,730 evaluations. 160 The two skill conditions (skill-reasoning, skill-execution) were run on all nine models 161 across the same three-population, three-replicate grid, producing a further 17,820 162 evaluations; Mistral Large 2 is excluded from the reported skill-arm aggregate as a rate-163 limit artefact (it returned usable output on only 3.7% of skill-arm cells; Methods), so the 164 headline skill figures are over eight models, and the all-nine-model aggregate is 165 reported in the supplement. On the common set of eight models for which all five 166 conditions yield valid output (Mistral fails only under the skill prompts), the constraint 167 gradient is unchanged: free-prompted 79.6%, retrieval-augmented 89.1%, skill-168 reasoning 95.5%, skill-execution 93.3% and the answer-supplied control 100%. Outputs 169 were scored on phenotype identification (A1), drug-specific recommendation (A2) and 170 lethal-class safety action (A3) by a rigorous rescorer with a justified clinical-equivalence 171 layer (Methods). 172 173 The five conditions form a gradient of constraint, ordered by how much correctness sits 174 in the model versus in the executed skill (Figure 2). 175

Retrieval failure modes are architectural, not knowledge gaps 211 

Two failure modes, absent from the other conditions, account for the retrieval 212 regression. First, drug substitution: because CPIC guidelines are keyed on gene rather 213 than gene-drug pair, a chunk retrieved for one drug carries recommendations for 214 several, and models echo the wrong one. Structured skills make capable models reliable and near model-invariant 231 

When the validated decision logic was provided as a structured skill and the model 232 applied it (skill-reasoning), phenotype accuracy rose to 95.5% (95% CI 95.1 to 96.0) 233 with three-of-three replicate reproducibility of 96.4% (eight-model, three-population, 234 three-replicate grid, Mistral excluded as a rate-limit artefact; Methods). The reasoning 235 models reached the ceiling: o3 and o4-mini attained 99.3 to 99.5% with near-perfect 236 reproducibility. Critically, the 27.9-percentage-point model spread of the free-prompted 237 condition collapsed to single digits (Figure 4): a structured skill scaffolds weaker models 238 up toward the strongest, so that model choice ceases to be the dominant determinant of 239 accuracy. Skill-reasoning, however, is not deterministic by construction: the clinical 240 answer still passes through the model’s generation, and a model’s stated reasoning is 241 not a faithful guarantee of the computation behind it (15), so it is reliable rather than 242 guaranteed. 243 244

Execution makes the clinical mapping exact and localises all residual error to 251 

input interpretation 252 

When the validated logic was executed as code, with the model confined to supplying a 253 structured input (skill-execution), the behaviour changed in kind. Executing the skill on 254 the correct diplotype produced the canonical phenotype, recommendation and safety 255 action on every one of the 110 cases (A1 = A2 = A3 = 100%, zero lethal-class errors in 256 this mapping-verification on the correct diplotype): the mapping is exact by construction. 257 End-to-end phenotype accuracy was 93.3% (95% CI 92.7 to 93.8; three-population, 258 three-replicate grid), and this number tracks, and is bounded below by, the accuracy of 259 the model’s input-interpretation step: whenever the model called the correct diplotype, 260 which it did on 92.0% of cells, the executed mapping was correct 100% of the time 261 (Figure 5); on the remaining 8.0% of cells, where the call was wrong, the executed 262 phenotype was nonetheless correct by coincidence in 15.8% of cases, so end-to-end 263 accuracy (93.3%) marginally exceeds the correct-call rate (92.0%). Skill-reasoning was marginally more accurate than skill-execution (95.5% 281 versus 93.3%) because, with the full rule table in context, a reasoning model can self-282 correct a loose input call, whereas execution commits to a single call and runs it rigidly. 283 This is the central trade and the central point: execution does not win on raw accuracy, 284 it wins on what clinical-grade requires, namely that the clinical mapping is provably 285 deterministic and auditable and that the residual risk is localised to one measurable step 286 rather than diffused across the model’s reasoning. 287 288 Skill-execution carries a higher lethal-class error rate than skill-reasoning (15.3% versus 289 8.5%), and the reason is the same trade that makes execution preferable. A reasoning 290 model holding the full rule table can silently correct a loose input call, so its lethal-class 291 residual is lower but unguaranteed and diffused across the model’s generation; 292 execution commits to the single called diplotype and runs it rigidly, so a wrong input call 293 propagates to a wrong, occasionally lethal, executed output. The residual is therefore 294 higher under execution but localised to one measurable step, auditable, and removable 295 by construction: a validated deterministic caller eliminates it at source and the abstain 296 path withholds the rest. Reasoning’s lower rate buys no such guarantee. We 297 recommend execution not because it minimises the raw lethal-class rate but because it 298 is the only condition whose residual can be bounded, monitored and driven to zero. 299 300 

Toward 100% correctness among emitted answers: a deterministic input caller 301 

Because the executed mapping is exact and the entire residual is the input call, the 302 route to a fully correct pipeline is to make the input call deterministic. Substituting a 303 validated diplotype caller, which maps raw variants to a diplotype with no generative 304 step, removes the only error source by construction; the agent abstains on no-calls and 305 ambiguous diplotypes. The real-genome arm here used PyPGx (0.26.0) as its reference 306 caller; PharmCAT (16) is an example of a deployment-grade caller suited to this role. 307 The achievable target is therefore not universal near-perfect clinical performance but a 308 bounded guarantee: 100% correctness among emitted in-scope answers, with explicit 309 abstention on no-calls, ambiguous alleles and any case outside the validated skill set. 310 We do not claim near-100% accuracy on arbitrary real-world genomes; the real-genome 311 arm below shows why such a claim would be false for model-mediated interpretation. 312 The model’s role contracts to what it does well and uniquely at scale: parsing 313 heterogeneous, natural-language input into the skill’s controlled vocabulary. 314 315

Discussion 421 

The five benchmarked conditions (free-prompted, retrieval-augmented, skill-reasoning, 422 skill-execution and an answer-supplied control) are not points on a single accuracy 423 gradient; they fail and succeed in qualitatively distinct ways, and ordered by how much 424 correctness sits in the model versus in the executed skill they tell one story. As 425 correctness moves out of the stochastic model and into the executed skill, accuracy 426 rises, model differences collapse, lethal-class errors fall, and, decisively, the residual 427 risk stops being diffuse and becomes localised, measurable and auditable. The 428 conceptual lesson is not that large language models are intrinsically unsafe for clinical 429 genomics, but that clinical-grade agentic implementations require correctness to reside 430 in versioned skills, not in the model. We organise the implications around three 431 questions: which principles generalise across genomics and biology, how to deploy a 432 validated skill trustworthily at scale, and how to develop such skills. 433 434 

What principles generalise across genomics and biology 435 

First, the trustworthy pattern is interpreter-plus-executed-skill, not reasoner. Scale wants 436 the model, because it absorbs the heterogeneity of real inputs that breaks rigid 437 pipelines; trust forbids the model from being the source of scientific truth, because 438 anything it generates is stochastic and unauditable. The resolution is a division of 439 labour: the model is the flexible natural-language interface and orchestrator, and 440 validated, versioned, executable skills are the deterministic scientific core. Our model-445 difference data make this concrete: the 27.9-point unconstrained spread collapsed 446 under the skill and, in the executed condition, vanished from the clinical mapping and 447 survived only at input interpretation (DeepSeek, for example, reasoned through the skill 448 at 96.4% but called the diplotype at 84.5%). Wherever agents are deployed in biology, 449 the fragile, model-dependent step is the conversion of messy reality into a structured 450 call (entity disambiguation, variant notation, units, ontology mapping, tool selection), and 451 that is where validation and monitoring belong. 452 453 Third, knowledge-in-context is necessary but not sufficient and can be actively harmful. 454 Retrieval, the field’s default clinical-grounding strategy, raised one metric while 455 increasing lethal-class errors, through architectural failure modes (drug substitution, 456 information-without-action) rather than knowledge gaps. Surfacing the correct evidence 457 does not produce the correct action; the decision logic must be encoded and executed, 458 not inferred from retrieved text. Crucially, this also rules out the simplest alternative 459 reading of the gradient, that it merely tracks how much information each condition is 460 given: the condition with the most information in context, retrieval, is the least safe, 461 while the two conditions given identical information, skill-reasoning and skill-execution, 462 differ not in accuracy but in whether their correctness is guaranteed. 463 464 Fourth, reproducibility and auditability must be engineered, not expected. Science 465 requires same-input-same-output and traceable provenance, which a generative model 466 cannot provide; the reproducible, auditable core must be executed code, with the model 467 confined to roles that are verified or non-load-bearing. Fifth, model-agnosticism is 468 achievable and valuable: under execution the clinical output was identical across 469 vendors and generations, so a validated skill can be revalidated once and run by any 470 compliant model, decoupling clinical safety from model churn, including the silent 471 behavioural drift documented for deployed models over time (17). Sixth, skills become 472 the unit of trustworthy accumulation: progress accrues as a growing library of validated, 473 executable, composable, independently auditable skills rather than as ever-larger 474 models trained to know more biology. 475 476 Finally, the honest limit is a spectrum of codifiability. Pharmacogenomics is unusually 477 tractable: discrete genotype, categorical phenotype, discrete action, a short guideline-478 codified mapping. The more a biological task reduces to a validatable executable 479 contract, the more trustworthy an agent can be on it; the less it does, the more the 480 agent’s outputs are hypotheses requiring human or experimental validation, with the 481 skill providing scaffolding and auditability rather than guaranteed correctness. That 482 spectrum tells the field where to deploy autonomous agents now (codifiable 483 interpretation: pharmacogenomics, variant classification against fixed criteria, 484 standardised QC, annotation) and where they remain assistive and exploratory 485 (discovery, evidence weighing under uncertainty, hypothesis generation). 486 487

How to deploy a validated skill at scale, I: the architecture for getting every 488 

emitted answer right 489 

The results specify a deployment architecture for running the skill across many 490 genomes agentically. Heterogeneous genomic input is normalised by the model into the 491 skill’s input schema; a validated deterministic caller, not the model, maps variants to a 492 diplotype, emitting an explicit no-call when ambiguous; the validated skill executes the 493 diplotype-to-phenotype-to-action mapping in code; deterministic validation gates cross-494 check the model’s parsed inputs against the caller before anything is emitted; and the 495 agent abstains and escalates to a human on no-calls, novel variants, or any gene-drug 496 pair outside the validated skill set. Because the mapping is model-invariant, the 497 orchestration model can be the cheapest available, or two models can be run on the 498 input step and required to agree as a free reliability gate. The honest definition of 499 success is that 100% of emitted responses are correct, with controlled abstention on the 500 irreducible residual; the residual is small, localised to input interpretation, and exactly 501 where quality control should sit. One cannot reach 100% by making the model reason 502 better, because the model’s input call tops out below 100% and varies by model; one 503 reaches it by executing the mapping, calling the input deterministically, validating the 504 handoff, and deferring the rest. 505 506 

How to deploy a validated skill at scale, II: the model as a controlled, monitored 507 

operator 508 

What makes a pipeline agentic, and what makes it trustworthy at scale, are operational 509 rather than cognitive. In the configuration that is reliable, the model does not perform 510 the science; it acts as an operator at the edges of the system. It ingests heterogeneous 511 real-world input (variant call files, free-text requests), normalises it into a skill’s 512 controlled vocabulary, selects the appropriate versioned skill from the library, and routes 513 the call to the deterministic caller and the executed mapping. The scientific computation 514 is executed code; the agent’s contribution is to run that code correctly, at high 515 throughput, under control, and to watch it. This is a deliberately smaller role for the 516 model than the field’s reflex assumes, and that contraction is the result, not a limitation: 517 the trustworthy agent is a constrained one. 518 519 Three operational properties make this trustworthy where unattended model reasoning 520 is not. First, control: every parsed input is validated against the deterministic caller 521 before anything is emitted; two inexpensive models can be required to agree on the 522 input call as a free reliability gate; and the agent abstains and escalates to a human on 523 no-calls, novel or uncertain-function alleles, and any gene-drug pair outside the 524 validated skill set. Because the executed mapping is exact, the only judgement the 525 agent must police is whether to emit or to defer. Second, monitoring: at the throughput 526 of a population biobank no human can audit individual calls, so the operator instruments 527 the process, tracking per-skill call volumes, abstention rates, the size and location of the 528 input-interpretation residual, and, critically, performance stratified by ancestry, so that a 529 population-specific degradation surfaces as a monitored signal rather than as 530 downstream harm. Third, adjustment: because the executed core is model-invariant, the 531 orchestration model is swappable and can be monitored for drift across versions without 532 revalidating the science. 533

534 Our real-genome results make the necessity of this loop concrete. When the model 535 performs the interpretation by reasoning, curated accuracy (96%) does not transfer to 536 real genomes and degrades with ancestry: it correctly reproduced the phenotype for 537 72% of European, 51% of Latin American and 40% of East African real diplotypes, with 538 abstention rising along the same gradient (5%, 26%, 36%) as the model met more 539 alleles outside its training, and with clinically incorrect calls most frequent for the East 540 African cohort. At population scale an unmonitored reasoning pipeline would emit these 541 as a large absolute number of plausible, undetected and inequitably distributed clinical 542 errors. These are precisely the errors a controlled, monitored agent gates: the 543 deterministic caller and the abstain path prevent the wrong call from being emitted, and 544 ancestry-stratified monitoring flags the residual before it reaches patients. High 545 throughput therefore does not relax the requirement for this architecture, it makes it 546 mandatory, because control and monitoring are the only mechanisms whose reliability is 547 a property of the system rather than of the model, and therefore the only ones that hold 548 as the agent is run across millions of diverse real genomes. 549 550 

How to develop agentic genomic skills 551 

For developers, the results change the unit of work from a prompt to a validated, 552 executable artefact. A skill should ship as four separable parts: a declarative 553 specification with provenance (source guideline and version, author, commit); a 554 deterministic core that encodes the validated mapping as code and is the thing 555 executed; a constrained input layer with an explicit controlled vocabulary and a 556 deterministic mapper from raw data to it; and a first-class abstain path. The model sits 557 only at the edges, normalising input and rendering output, never producing the result. 558 The development workflow follows: encode the authoritative source as structured rules 559 rather than prose; write the deterministic core test-first and require it to pass exactly; 560 build or wrap the deterministic input mapper, because that is where the residual lives; 561 add the model interface last, with outputs constrained to the schema and cross-checked 562 against the deterministic components; and engineer abstention and provenance 563 explicitly, versioning and regression-testing the skill against guideline updates as one 564 would clinical decision-support software. Two consequences follow: maintain two test 565 suites, one for logic correctness (deterministic, must pass exactly, validated without any 566 model) and one for input-interpretation robustness (measured and monitored, accepted 567 below 100% and bounded by validation and abstention); and treat the model as a 568 swappable, monitored component, since the executed core is model-invariant. 569 570 

Limitations 571 

The benchmark uses 110 synthetic cases with genotypes specified as text rather than 572 variant call format files; we evaluate the analytical layer only. Phenotype accuracy, the headline metric, is drug-577 independent and so is unaffected by this asymmetry. Outlook 598 

The immediate extensions are to integrate a validated deterministic caller and 599 demonstrate 100% correctness among emitted in-scope answers with controlled 600 abstention on the residual; to scale the skill conditions to the full population and 601 replicate grid; and to test skill composition and selection across multi-step workflows. 602 The same architecture should extend to variant interpretation, where the mapping is 603 codified in ClinGen and ACMG-AMP guidance, to polygenic risk interpretation, and to 604 rare-disease triage, where the validation properties demonstrated here must hold at 605 every step of a longer chain. 606 607
